ABB

ABB (ASEA Brown Boveri) is a Swedish-Swiss multinational corporation headquartered in Zürich, Switzerland, operating mainly in robotics and the power and automation technology areas. It is one of the largest engineering companies as well as one of the largest conglomerates in the world, with operations in around 100 countries.

One of its divisions, Industrial Automation, has as a main goal of providing customers with products and solutions for instrumentation, automation and optimization of industrial processes. The industries served include:

  • Oil and Gas
  • Power
  • Chemicals and Pharmaceuticals
  • Pulp and Paper
  • Metals and Minerals
  • Marine
  • Turbocharging

Key customer benefits include improved asset productivity and energy savings.

Also, the division consists of seven (07) business units:

  • Control Technologies (the world's number 1 DCS supplier)
  • Marine and Ports
  • Measurement and Analytics
  • Oil, Gas and Chemicals
  • Power Generation and Water
  • Process Industries
  • Turbocharging

In the CT arena, one of the main products are Distributed Control Systems, which implement DCS products, solutions, services for continuous or batch process industries, infrastructure, energy and utilities, all delivered in collaborative control rooms.

One of these DCS solutions is System 800xA, a system for process monitoring and efficient control with the capacity to improve engineering efficiency, operator performance and asset utilization.

Key functional areas within System 800xA are:

  • Operations
  • Batch Management
  • Manufacturing Management
  • Information Management
  • Control and I/O
  • Engineering
  • Asset Optimization
  • Device Management & Fieldbuses

The functional area Control and I/O includes, among others, the hardware platform AC 800M, together with the interfaces, modules and associated units that are used in the AC 800M controller.
